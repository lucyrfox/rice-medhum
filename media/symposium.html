Grey Matter: Controversies in Medicine

Join Rice Medical Humanities as we explore a wide range of controversies in medicine for our second annual symposium! Grey Matter: Controversies in Medicine will feature a diverse set of speakers from Baylor College of Medicine and University of Texas Health Science Center.

Pictures and videos from the 2014 Symposium will be posted soon!


 
 

About the Symposium

From lectures on the ethics of healthcare for undocumented patients, to the discrepancy between the supposed and actual focus on humanities in medical education, to the ethics of prenatal genomic testing, our speakers' lectures will be sure to spark your interest in the medical humanities.

Lunch will be provided prior to the lectures, which start at noon sharp, and a small reception will conclude the event.

Register to attend ($5) at http://signup.rice.edu/GreyMatter (If you wish to register with cash, please contact us Julia Chavez at jc81@rice.edu, Margo Fendrich at mlf2@rice.edu, or Ashley Phillips at alp5@rice.edu.) ***REGISTRATION ENDS 6:00 PM ON FRIDAY***

Lecture Schedule:

12:00 PM- 12:50 PM “Autonomous’ Decisions and the Ethics of Nudging” with Dr. Jennifer Blumenthal-Barby, BCM

1:00 PM- 1:40 PM “Navigating Ethical Complexities and Disputes in the Hospital: The Work of a Clinical Ethicist” with Dr. Courtenay Bruce, BCM

1:50 PM- 2:30 PM “Health Promotion for People with Type 1 Diabetes: Care vs Cure” with Dr. Jake Kushner, BCM, Texas Children's

2:40 PM- 3:20 PM “Medical Humanism: Hear What They Say, See What They Do” with Dr. Adan Rios, UTHealth,

3:30 PM- 4:10 PM “Deep Trouble: Two Preimplantation Genetic Diagnosis- Related Controversies” with Dr. Mary A Majumder, BCM

4:20 PM- 5:00 PM “Ethical Dilemma of Medical Care to Illegal Immigrants: A Doctor’s Perspective” with Dr. Laila Woc Colburn, BCM, National School of Tropical Medicine

We'll see you there!
